Basics of Scales

Scales are defined as any consecutive series of notes that are arranged by ascending or descending order of pitch.

Scales are the framework of music. They help set the boundaries or rules of how to play certain chords and progressions of notes.

The most widely used scales in Western music are major and minor scales. We will only be looking at major scales.

Sharps and Flats

There are 12 major scales. C, G, D, A, E, B, F# or Gb, Db, Ab, Eb, Bb, and F.

A sharp note, such as F#, means you play one half step above the note. In this case you would play a half step above F.

A flat note, such as Bb, means you play one half step below the note. In this case you would play a half step below B.
